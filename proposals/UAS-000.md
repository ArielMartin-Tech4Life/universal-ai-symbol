# UAS-000

## Universal AI Symbol

**Identifier:** UAS-000

**Title:** Motivation for a Universal Symbol Representing Artificial Intelligence

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Approved

**Created:** 2026

---

# Abstract

Artificial Intelligence has become part of everyday life.

People interact with AI through education, medicine, engineering, creativity, science, communication, entertainment, research, and countless other fields.

Despite this growing presence, there is no simple, neutral, and universally recognizable symbol that represents Artificial Intelligence itself.

This proposal introduces the motivation for creating an open symbol that anyone may freely use, modify, reproduce, or distribute without permission.

The purpose of this document is not to define the symbol itself, but to explain why such a symbol may be valuable.

---

# Motivation

Human beings naturally communicate through symbols.

Some symbols represent emotions.

Others represent ideas.

Others represent actions.

A symbol can communicate meaning faster than words and can transcend language barriers.

Today, Artificial Intelligence is commonly represented by illustrations of robots, brains, computer chips, or electronic circuits.

These representations describe particular technologies or artistic interpretations rather than the broader concept of Artificial Intelligence.

As AI continues to become part of everyday human life, an opportunity exists to propose a simple, neutral, and open visual representation.

---

# Problem Statement

There is currently no widely adopted visual symbol that represents Artificial Intelligence in a general, technology-independent, and organization-independent way.

Most existing imagery is tied to specific products, companies, artistic styles, or hardware concepts.

This makes it difficult to refer to Artificial Intelligence as a concept using a single visual language comparable to other universally recognized symbols.

---

# Goals

This project aims to:

* Propose a simple visual symbol representing Artificial Intelligence.
* Keep the symbol independent of any company, product, government, or organization.
* Make the symbol freely available to everyone.
* Encourage voluntary adoption without imposing its use.
* Document the design process in an open and transparent manner.
* Preserve the project's history in a public repository.

---

# Non-Goals

This project does not aim to:

* Declare an official global symbol.
* Replace existing logos or trademarks.
* Represent any specific AI model, company, or technology.
* Require anyone to adopt the symbol.
* Restrict derivative works or commercial use.

---

# Openness

The proposed symbol is intended to belong to everyone.

No permission should be required to use it.

No royalties should be required.

No exclusive ownership is intended.

The project embraces openness as a fundamental principle, allowing individuals, communities, organizations, educators, researchers, artists, developers, and companies to adopt the symbol if they find it useful.

---

# Historical Record

This repository serves as the historical record of the project's development.

Its purpose is to preserve the evolution of the proposal through public documentation rather than through ownership.

The history of the project remains available to anyone interested in understanding how the proposal originated and evolved.

Future documents in the UAS series will define:

---

# Conclusion

Symbols become meaningful through use.

This project does not seek to assign meaning by authority.

Instead, it offers an open proposal that anyone may freely adopt if it proves useful.

Whether the symbol becomes widely recognized will ultimately be determined by the people who choose to use it.

The role of this project is simply to make that possibility freely available.
