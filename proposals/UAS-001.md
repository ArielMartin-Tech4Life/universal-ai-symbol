# Abstract

This document presents the first public symbol proposal for the Universal AI Symbol project.

The proposal introduces an abstract visual symbol intended to represent Artificial Intelligence independently of any company, product, government, hardware platform, or software implementation.

The proposal is published openly so that it may be discussed, improved, adopted, or replaced through an open and transparent process.

---

# Background

Artificial Intelligence has rapidly become part of everyday life.

Despite its growing importance, there is still no simple visual symbol that people can use to represent AI in a neutral and technology-independent way.

Most current representations rely on illustrations of robots, brains, computer chips, or futuristic machines.

While these images are familiar, they describe particular interpretations of AI rather than the broader concept itself.

This proposal explores an alternative approach.

---

# Design Overview

The proposed symbol is intentionally abstract.

Rather than depicting a machine, a human brain, or electronic hardware, it uses a continuous geometric form that can be interpreted without being restricted to a single meaning.

Its simplicity allows people to associate their own understanding with the symbol while maintaining a recognizable visual identity.

---

# Visual Interpretation

The proposal intentionally avoids assigning a single official meaning.

However, several natural interpretations emerge from its geometry.

It may suggest:

- curiosity
- continuous learning
- knowledge
- dialogue
- growth
- evolution
- exploration
- connection
- collaboration
- intelligence

These interpretations are descriptive rather than prescriptive.

People remain free to interpret the symbol according to their own experiences.

---

# Design Characteristics

The proposal was designed to satisfy the principles established in the project's Design Principles document.

Its most important characteristics include:

- abstract rather than literal
- simple geometry
- continuous stroke
- recognizable silhouette
- scalable
- drawable by hand
- suitable for engraving
- suitable for embroidery
- suitable for digital interfaces
- suitable for three-dimensional modeling
- naturally animatable

# Known Limitations

As the first public proposal, this symbol has several limitations.

Its geometry has not yet been formally standardized.

Accessibility testing has not yet been completed.

Cross-cultural interpretation remains to be studied.

Long-term recognition can only emerge through voluntary adoption over time.

These limitations are expected for an early proposal and should be addressed through future revisions.

# Proposal Status

This document intentionally presents a proposal rather than a finalized standard.

Future revisions may refine or improve the symbol while preserving the project's founding principles.

Any future proposal should be evaluated according to the same public design principles established by this project.

---

# Conclusion

A symbol becomes meaningful through use rather than declaration.

This proposal offers an open candidate for representing Artificial Intelligence.

Its future depends entirely on whether individuals, communities, educators, researchers, artists, developers, organizations, and companies find it useful enough to adopt voluntarily.
