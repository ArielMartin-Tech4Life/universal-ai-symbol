# Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

## Universal AI Symbol Project

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Approved

**Created:** 2026

---

# What is the Universal AI Symbol?

The Universal AI Symbol is an open proposal for a visual symbol representing Artificial Intelligence as a general concept.

The project explores the possibility of creating a simple, neutral, and freely available symbol that is independent of any company, product, technology, or implementation.

---

# Is this the official global symbol for Artificial Intelligence?

No.

The project does not claim to create or declare an official global symbol.

Symbols become meaningful through voluntary adoption by people and communities over time.

This project only provides an open proposal.

---

# Who owns the Universal AI Symbol?

No exclusive ownership is intended.

The symbol and related materials are released under the Creative Commons CC0 1.0 Universal dedication.

The goal is to allow anyone to use, study, modify, reproduce, and distribute the symbol freely.

---

# Can I use the symbol?

Yes.

Anyone may use the symbol for personal, educational, artistic, research, commercial, or non-commercial purposes.

No permission is required.

No attribution is required.

---

# Can companies use the symbol?

Yes.

Companies and organizations may use the symbol if they find it useful.

The symbol is not associated with any specific company, product, service, or AI system.

---

# Can I modify the symbol?

Yes.

The project allows modifications and derivative works under the principles of openness established by the CC0 dedication.

However, modified versions should not be presented as the original official proposal unless they are documented as a separate proposal.

---

# Why create a symbol for Artificial Intelligence?

Humanity has always used symbols to communicate ideas that are larger than words.

Artificial Intelligence has become part of education, science, engineering, creativity, communication, and daily life.

This project explores the possibility of creating a simple visual language for referring to AI as a concept.

---

# Does the symbol represent robots, computers, or machines?

No.

The symbol intentionally avoids representing a specific technology.

It does not represent robots, computer chips, brains, companies, or any particular implementation of Artificial Intelligence.

It represents the broader concept.

---

# What does the symbol mean?

The project does not impose a single interpretation.

The symbol was designed to allow different people and cultures to associate their own understanding with it.

Possible interpretations may include:

* curiosity
* learning
* evolution
* connection
* exploration
* intelligence
* collaboration

These are inspirations, not restrictions.

---

# How was the symbol designed?

The symbol follows the project's design principles:

* simplicity
* drawability
* scalability
* neutrality
* memorability
* reproducibility
* universality
* openness

The design process is documented publicly in this repository.

No.

# What determines whether the symbol becomes universal?

No individual, organization, or project can decide that a symbol becomes universal.

Recognition comes from voluntary use.

The future of the symbol depends on whether people find it useful enough to adopt.
