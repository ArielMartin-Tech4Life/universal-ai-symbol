# Universal AI Symbol Geometry

**Document:** GEOMETRY.md

**Version:** 1.0

**Status:** Draft

---

# Purpose

This document defines the geometric philosophy of the Universal AI Symbol.

The goal is to ensure that the symbol can be reproduced consistently while remaining simple enough to be drawn by hand.

Exact mathematical construction may evolve in future revisions, but the core geometry should remain recognizable.

---

# Geometric Principles

The symbol is based on the following principles:

* continuous stroke
* smooth curves
* balanced proportions
* visual symmetry
* no unnecessary details
* no disconnected elements

Every visible line should contribute to the overall identity of the symbol.

---

# Construction Philosophy

The symbol should appear as if it could be drawn naturally in one continuous movement.

Its geometry should avoid sharp interruptions, unnecessary complexity, or decorative elements.

The visual flow should communicate continuity rather than fragmentation.

---

# Visual Balance

The overall composition should remain visually balanced.

No section of the symbol should dominate the others.

The visual weight should feel centered regardless of orientation or scale.

---

# Scalability

The geometry should remain recognizable at:

* icon size
* mobile interface size
* printed documents
* posters
* signage
* large architectural applications

---

# Reproducibility

A person with no special artistic skills should be able to reproduce a recognizable version of the symbol after observing it briefly.

Perfect precision is not required for recognition.

---

# Future Refinement

Future revisions of this document may include:

* construction grid
* reference circles
* proportional ratios
* stroke radius
* curvature guidelines
* vector construction

These refinements should clarify the geometry without changing the visual identity of the symbol.

---

# Principle

Geometry exists to preserve recognition, not to introduce unnecessary complexity.
